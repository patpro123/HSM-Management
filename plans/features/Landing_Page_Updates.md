# **The HSM Method**
## *5 Daily Habits of Students Who Score ‘Distinction’ in Trinity College London Exams*
### **Introduction: The 167-Hour Reality Check**
Every year, thousands of parents enroll their children in music lessons, hoping for brilliant results. Yet, the vast majority of students stall, get frustrated, and eventually quit.
Why? Because standard music schools focus entirely on the **1 hour a week** the student spends with an instructor.

We focus on training our students for the **other 167 hours of the week** when they are at home.
Through thousands of hours of elite coaching, our faculty—**Joshua, Subroto, Isaac Lawrence, and Sangeetha**—have isolated the exact 5 daily micro-habits that separate a struggling student from a **Distinction-grade performer**.
Here is the exact blueprint your child can use to unlock their musical potential.
## **Habit 1: The "Rule of Zero Friction" (Setting the Stage)**
> *“Distinction students don’t practice more because they have more willpower; they practice more because their instrument is impossible to ignore.”*
>
* **The Trap:** Most parents pack their child's guitar away in a heavy zippered gig bag after class, or pile heavy text books on top of the keyboard lid. When it's time to practice, the child has to uncover, unpack, setup, and plug in the gear. That minor friction is enough to make a child choose video games instead.
* **The HSM Method:** Top-tier students keep their instruments out, tuned, and completely uncovered in a dedicated "music corner" of their room. The book stand is open to the correct page. The drumsticks are sitting right on the snare pad.
* **The Goal:** Reduce the physical time it takes to start playing to **less than 10 seconds**.
## **Habit 2: The 20-Minute Micro-Dose (Consistency Over Cramming)**
> *“20 minutes of daily deliberate practice builds muscle memory 7x faster than a frantic 2-hour jam session the night before class.”*
>
* **The Trap:** The classic Sunday-night panic. The student hasn't touched their instrument all week, so they try to cram a week's worth of learning into one long, grueling session. This causes mental burnout and physical hand fatigue, and none of the skills stick.
* **The HSM Method:** Our brains build *myelin* (the insulation around neural pathways that creates muscle memory) through frequent, short repetitions. Distinction students track their progress daily using the **HSM Daily 10 Habit Tracker**. They focus on short, hyper-focused 20-minute daily sessions.
* **The Goal:** Establish a daily streak. 6 days of 20-minute practices will always beat 1 day of a 120-minute practice.
## **Habit 3: Extreme Isolation (Slowing Down to Speed Up)**
> *“Amateurs practice a song from start to finish until they get it right. Professionals isolate the hardest 2 bars and repeat them until they can't get them wrong.”*
>
* **The Trap:** When a student sits down, they instinctively play the parts of the Trinity piece they are already good at (usually the intro). When they hit a difficult chord change or a complex rhythm bridge, they stumble through it, make a mistake, disregard it, and keep playing to the end. They are essentially practicing their mistakes.
* **The HSM Method:** Our faculty teaches students to use "micro-targeting." If bars 9 and 10 of a piece are problematic, the student turns off the rest of the song. They loop just those two bars at half-speed with a metronome, repeating it perfectly 5 times before moving on.
* **The Goal:** Never practice an entire song if only two bars are broken. Isolate, fix, and re-integrate.
## **Habit 4: Closing the Feedback Loop (Self-Recording)**
> *“The ear is a poor judge while the fingers are working. The camera never lies.”*
>
* **The Trap:** While playing, a student's brain is completely overwhelmed with tracking finger positions, reading notation, and keeping time. They literally cannot hear the subtle mistakes they are making in tone, timing, or vocal pitch.
* **The HSM Method:** Distinction students treat practice like an elite athlete treats game tape. During their daily 10 micro-habits, they spend 60 seconds setting up a smartphone to record a quick audio or video clip of their performance. When they play it back, they instantly notice things like bad posture, rushed timing, or improper wrist bowing.
* **The Goal:** Record one snippet a day. Self-correction is the fastest shortcut to an examiner's praise.
## **Habit 5: The Academic Reality Check (The Assessment Anchor)**
> *“Progress that isn't measured simply isn't happening.”*
>
* **The Trap:** Students and parents often operate in a blind spot, guessing if they are ready for a global Trinity Exam based on "how the song sounds" at home.
* **The HSM Method:** Distinction students never guess. They rely on the rigid data provided inside the **HSM Student Portal**. Every single month, HSM instructors perform formalized assessments on technical scales, sight-reading, and performance pieces, grading them against strict Trinity criteria. Top students actively check their assessment portals to see exactly where their weaknesses lie.
* **The Goal:** Treat music like an elite academic subject. Use data to map out the exact path from Grade 1 to Grade 8.
